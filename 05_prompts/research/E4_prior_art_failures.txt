Prior Art E4 — Civic Infrastructure Transparency Failures

Research civic tech projects in the infrastructure transparency space that did not succeed — either they were built and not maintained, not adopted, or did not reach the residents they were designed for.

Investigate:
- What are the most common reasons civic infrastructure transparency tools fail after launch?
- Are there documented examples of city project maps or service status tools that went stale or were abandoned?
- What organizational or political factors lead to civic tech tools being discontinued?
- What data maintenance requirements are most commonly underestimated?
- Are there specific technology choices (scraping, manual updates, single-vendor APIs) that create fragility?

Apply to Richmond context: what failure modes are most likely for a tool built in this hackathon, given the known constraints (GPS not ready, no continuation pathway named, limited staff capacity for updates)?

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Top 3 failure modes most relevant to this pillar
- Design and handoff recommendations to mitigate each failure mode
