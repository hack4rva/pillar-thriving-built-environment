User Research B5 — Accessibility-Dependent Residents

Research the specific needs of Richmond residents who rely on accessible streets and sidewalks: residents with disabilities, elderly residents, and transit-dependent residents. These users are most severely affected by infrastructure disruptions and have the least ability to adapt.

Investigate:
- How do construction projects affect accessible routes (sidewalk closures, curb cut removal, detour routes)?
- How do street cleaning and snow removal priorities affect mobility-impaired residents (delayed clearing of accessible routes)?
- What information do accessibility-dependent residents need that general residents do not?
- What are the digital access barriers for this population (low tech literacy, screen reader compatibility, limited smartphone access)?
- Are there any existing Richmond programs or contacts for accessibility in public works?

Apply to the hackathon context: what accessibility considerations should teams building an infrastructure visibility tool or fleet status tool incorporate from the start?

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Specific design requirements for accessibility-inclusive tools
- Richmond-specific context where available
