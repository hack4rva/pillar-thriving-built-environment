Risk G4 — Data Freshness and Staleness Risks

Research the risk of stale or infrequently-updated project data undermining the value and credibility of an infrastructure visibility tool.

Investigate:
- How frequently do ArcGIS layers maintained by city GIS departments typically update?
- What is the typical lag between a project status change in an internal DPW system and a GeoHub layer update?
- How do the best civic infrastructure tools communicate data freshness to users (last updated dates, staleness warnings)?
- What happens to resident trust when a tool shows a project as "active" when it has actually been completed for months?
- How should a prototype handle a situation where the data source hasn't updated in 30+ days?

Specific to this pillar:
- What update frequency should a team design against for GeoHub project data?
- What is the right threshold for displaying a "data may be outdated" warning?
- How should the tool handle projects that are no longer in the data source (completed and removed) vs. projects with no recent updates?

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Data freshness UX recommendations
- Recommended cache strategy for a weekend-built prototype
