Problem Landscape A4 — National Context for Public Works Transparency Failures

Provide national context for why public works information transparency fails in American cities. Research:

- What are the most documented reasons residents cannot find information about local infrastructure projects?
- How common is the problem of project data fragmentation across ArcGIS, program pages, and grant documentation?
- What research or reporting exists on the civic costs of poor infrastructure communication (resident frustration, 311 volume, trust erosion)?
- What has been written about the gap between how cities produce project data (for procurement and engineering) and how residents need to consume it (plain language, address-based)?
- Are there any national standards or best practices for public infrastructure communication?

Apply findings to the Richmond context where relevant.

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Key citations a team could use to justify their project to judges
