Top 10 Research Questions — A Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment

For each question, provide verifiable findings with source titles, clearly labeled inferences, and unknowns.

1. Which specific layers on Richmond GeoHub contain transportation or infrastructure project data that is publicly accessible via the ArcGIS REST API?

2. What DPW datasets exist on the Richmond Open Data Portal (data.richmondgov.com) that could support an infrastructure project lookup tool?

3. How do cities with successful infrastructure project transparency tools (NYC DOT, Portland PBOT, Boston) structure their public-facing project data? What fields are essential?

4. What are the most common reasons residents in mid-sized cities cannot find information about active construction projects near their homes?

5. What does a "GPS fleet management system being installed" typically mean operationally for a city DPW department? What data will it produce when complete?

6. How do Boston's Winter Ready and Chicago's Plow Tracker differ in their data requirements? Which approach is achievable without real-time GPS?

7. What is the current state of public works communication on rva.gov? How many clicks does it take a Richmond resident to find information about a specific transportation project?

8. What open source or low-cost tools exist for building an address-based project lookup tool using ArcGIS REST API data?

9. How do cities handle the translation of technical engineering project descriptions into plain language for public-facing communication?

10. What are the most common failure modes of civic infrastructure transparency tools — why do they go stale, get ignored, or fail to reach the residents who need them most?
