Prior Art E1 — City Infrastructure Project Tracker Tools Nationally

Research the best examples of public-facing city infrastructure and transportation project tracker tools in the United States.

For each example, describe:
- City and tool name
- What it does (project types covered, search capability, status display)
- Data source (ArcGIS, open data portal, custom API)
- Key UX features (address search, map view, status filter, notification)
- Whether it has plain-language descriptions
- Known strengths and limitations
- Relevance to Richmond's problem

Cities to investigate specifically:
- NYC Department of Transportation capital projects
- Portland Bureau of Transportation projects
- Boston street improvements
- Chicago infrastructure viewer
- Louisville Metro public works
- Philadelphia Streets Department
- Any Virginia peer cities (Arlington, Alexandria, Norfolk)

Output:
- Comparison table: city, tool name, key features, data source, strengths, limitations
- Top 2–3 models most relevant to Richmond
- Key UX patterns to adopt
- Key mistakes to avoid
