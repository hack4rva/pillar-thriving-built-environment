Risk G2 — Building on GPS Infrastructure That Doesn't Fully Exist Yet

Research the specific risk of building a fleet operations tool that is designed around GPS data that is not yet available.

Investigate:
- What happens when a tool is built against a synthetic data schema and the real data arrives in a different format?
- How should a hackathon team document their synthetic schema to maximize compatibility with future real data?
- What is the reputational risk if a fleet tracking demo is presented without clear "mock data" labeling?
- How do judges and City officials typically react to prototypes built on synthetic data when disclosed clearly?
- Are there successful examples of civic tech tools that used synthetic data in their prototype phase and later connected to real data?

Specific to this pillar:
- What are the exact phrases a team should use when describing their fleet tool to judges?
- What documentation should be created during the hackathon to make the tool ready for real GPS data?
- If the GPS schema turns out to be different from the synthetic schema, what is the rework effort?

Output:
- Risk assessment with likelihood and severity
- Documentation strategy for synthetic GPS schema
- Exact demo language recommended for disclosing synthetic data clearly
- Schema design recommendations that maximize forward compatibility
