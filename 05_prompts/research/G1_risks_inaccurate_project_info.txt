Risk G1 — Inaccurate Project Information and Timeline Claims

Research the risk of displaying inaccurate project timelines or status in a public-facing tool, and how to mitigate it responsibly.

Investigate:
- How often do infrastructure project timelines change after public announcement?
- What are the most common causes of timeline changes (weather, contractor issues, design changes, permitting delays)?
- What harm can result from a resident acting on an incorrect project end date (parking plans, business decisions, travel routing)?
- How do cities with established project tracker tools handle timeline changes in their public-facing data?
- What is the legal or liability exposure for a non-official tool that displays incorrect project information?

Recommend:
- What disclaimers should be displayed on any tool showing project timelines?
- How should stale data be handled (how long before a "last updated" date triggers a staleness warning)?
- What is the right language for "estimated completion" vs. "scheduled completion" vs. "completion date" in a public-facing display?

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Specific disclaimer language recommended
- Data staleness handling recommendation
