Opportunities F5 — What NOT to Build

Research and reason about which solution directions are explicitly out of scope, too risky, too dependent on unavailable infrastructure, or likely to mislead residents or judges.

Do not build or recommend:

1. Real-time GPS vehicle tracking: The GPS infrastructure is explicitly incomplete as of March 2026. Any tool claiming to show vehicle locations in real time would be built on non-existent data. Research why this is harmful beyond just being inaccurate.

2. Sustainability Design Standards enforcement or compliance tools: The working session explicitly noted this requires more internal City work. Research why this category fails in hackathon contexts specifically.

3. DPW system integration tools: Tools that would require read or write access to DPW's internal project management, scheduling, or personnel systems. Research the authorization and security barriers.

4. Predictive project completion tools: Any tool that claims to predict when a project will be done based on current data. Why is this dangerous without official source confirmation?

5. Resident eligibility or service determination tools: Any tool that tells a resident whether they qualify for a service or program based on address or income. Why is this scope-creep in this problem space?

Output:
- For each "do not build" category: the specific risk it poses
- How to redirect a team that proposes one of these
- How to address each in demo if judges ask "why didn't you build X?"
