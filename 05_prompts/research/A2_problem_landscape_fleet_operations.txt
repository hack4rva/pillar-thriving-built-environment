Problem Landscape A2 — Fleet Operations Manual Reporting Problem

Investigate the manual reporting problem in snow removal and street cleaning operations for a mid-size city DPW department. Using verifiable public information and clearly labeled inferences:

- How do cities currently track route completion during storm events without GPS?
- What are the operational costs of manual check-in (radio calls, phone calls, whiteboard tracking)?
- How does the lack of route completion visibility affect supervisor decision-making during a snowstorm?
- How does the disconnect between operations and communications affect resident-facing public updates?
- What is the minimum data needed to provide useful public status updates (zone-level vs. street-level vs. vehicle-level)?

Apply findings to Richmond's situation where GPS installation is in progress but not complete.

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns
- Minimum viable data specification for a useful fleet status tool
