Risk G3 — Accessibility Risks in Infrastructure Visibility Tools

Research the accessibility risks in map-based and SMS-based infrastructure visibility tools — particularly how they may exclude residents who need the information most.

Investigate:
- What percentage of Richmond residents lack reliable smartphone access or broadband at home?
- How do map-based interfaces (Leaflet, Mapbox) perform for screen reader users?
- What are the WCAG requirements for a public-facing civic tool displaying spatial information?
- How do SMS-based notification systems exclude residents without mobile phones or those with prepaid plans?
- Are there any documented cases where a civic infrastructure tool reached only highly educated or high-income residents, defeating its equity purpose?

Recommend:
- Minimum accessibility requirements for a weekend-built prototype
- What to avoid (inaccessible map interactions, color-only status indicators, no keyboard navigation)
- How to make an address lookup tool accessible without a full screen reader overhaul in 48 hours
- Equity framing for the demo: how to acknowledge the gap between the tool's current accessibility and its full potential reach

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Minimum accessibility checklist for this pillar's prototypes
- Demo language for addressing accessibility questions from judges
