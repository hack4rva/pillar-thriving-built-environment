User Research B1 — The Concerned Resident

Build a detailed user persona for a Richmond resident who wants to understand infrastructure projects happening in their neighborhood. Research:

- What motivates residents to seek out project information (curiosity, disruption, parking, access, safety)?
- What are the typical digital literacy levels and device preferences for this user group across Richmond's diverse neighborhoods?
- How do residents currently discover that a project is happening (physical signs, word of mouth, social media, news)?
- What specific questions do residents most commonly ask about construction projects (how long, who is responsible, can I park here, will my water be off)?
- Are there equity considerations — do residents in lower-income neighborhoods or non-English-speaking households face additional barriers to finding project information?
- What would "good enough" look like for this user — what is the minimum information that answers their question?

Output:
- Persona sketch with motivations, barriers, and key questions
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Equity considerations specific to Richmond's demographics
