Problem Landscape A1 — Infrastructure Visibility Reality

Investigate the real-world shape of Richmond's infrastructure project visibility problem. Using only verifiable public information and clearly labeled inferences, explain where the difficulty actually occurs for a Richmond resident trying to find information about construction or public works projects near their home.

Focus areas:
- What paths exist for a resident to find project information today (City website, GeoHub, 311, etc.)?
- How many steps does it take? What barriers appear at each step?
- What role does technical language play in the accessibility problem?
- Are there Richmond-specific examples of this problem (known projects with poor public communication)?

Output:
- Facts (with source titles)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns (what cannot be verified publicly)
- Assessment of primary friction points for Richmond residents
