Core Problem Framing J1 — Academic Frameworks for Public Service Transparency Gaps

Conduct a comprehensive literature review on the academic and theoretical frameworks used to describe the lack of transparency in public service delivery systems — particularly urban infrastructure services such as power restoration, sanitation, road maintenance, sewer repair, and emergency dispatch.

Focus areas:

1. Information Asymmetry in Public Services
   - How does the concept of information asymmetry (Akerlof, Stiglitz) apply to government-to-citizen service delivery?
   - What are the documented effects of delayed, fragmented, or opaque data flows on citizen trust, service efficiency, and operational outcomes?
   - Are there quantitative studies measuring the trust deficit caused by service opacity?

2. Civic Visibility Gaps
   - Is there an established academic term or framework for the gap between what governments know about service operations and what citizens can see?
   - What models exist for "service opacity" or "civic information asymmetry"?
   - How do researchers distinguish between data that exists internally but is not shared vs. data that does not exist at all?

3. Theoretical Models
   - Principal-agent theory applied to municipal service delivery
   - Public value theory (Moore) and how transparency creates or destroys public value
   - E-government maturity models that include transparency stages
   - Open government and open data frameworks (OGP, Sunlight Foundation principles)
   - Digital-era governance (Dunleavy) and its predictions about service visibility

4. Measurement Frameworks
   - How is public service transparency measured in academic literature?
   - What indices or scorecards exist (e.g., Open Budget Index, open data indices)?
   - How do researchers measure citizen satisfaction with service communication?

Output:
- Key academic frameworks and their originators (with paper/book titles)
- Definitions of relevant terminology (information asymmetry, service opacity, civic visibility gap, etc.)
- Summary of empirical findings on the trust/efficiency impact of poor service visibility
- Gaps in the literature — what has NOT been studied
- Recommended reading list (top 10 papers/books) for a team building a civic transparency tool
