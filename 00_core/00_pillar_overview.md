# Pillar Overview — A Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment

## Purpose
This repository is a markdown knowledge base for teams working on the A Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment pillar in the Richmond Civic Hackathon. It helps teams understand the problem space, review problem statements, explore public data, and shape realistic weekend MVPs.

## Pillar framing
A Thriving and Sustainable Built Environment focuses on helping Richmonders understand and navigate the physical infrastructure that shapes daily life, including:
- infrastructure project transparency (knowing what is being built, where, and why)
- plain-language access to transportation and construction project information
- neighborhood-level awareness of scheduled and active public works
- fleet operations visibility for services like snow removal and street cleaning
- resident self-service for understanding when their street will be serviced

## Why this matters
A well-functioning built environment depends on public trust and communication:
- residents cannot plan around construction they cannot find information about
- DPW staff spend time answering questions that a clear public resource could deflect
- snow removal and street cleaning gaps erode public confidence in City services
- fragmented data across ArcGIS, program pages, and grant documentation makes even interested residents give up
- equitable access to infrastructure information matters most for residents with fewer workaround options

## Key themes from the working session (February 26, 2026)
Three themes emerged from the pillar committee working session:

### Theme 1: Transportation Project Visibility (strong hackathon fit)
Residents struggle to find and understand infrastructure project information. Data exists across ArcGIS, grant documentation, and program pages but is fragmented and technically written. Rubric score: 25/32 Strong.

### Theme 2: Fleet Operations Visibility (practical but data-constrained)
DPW supervisors and residents both lack timely information about route progress during snow removal and street cleaning. GPS infrastructure is being installed but is not yet complete. Any solution must account for this constraint. Rubric score: 22/32 Needs work (D3=2).

### Theme 3: Sustainability Integration (important but not yet hackable)
The Sustainable Design Standards problem is strategically important but requires more internal City workflow coordination before a hackathon solution could be effective. Teams should not pursue this theme this weekend.

## Useful prototype directions
Prototypes that often help in this pillar:
- make public project information easier to find by address or neighborhood
- translate technical DPW project descriptions into plain language
- aggregate project data from multiple sources into one view
- provide schedule-based service status for snow removal and street cleaning zones
- alert residents when projects in their area change status

## Less useful directions
Prototypes that tend to struggle in a weekend:
- require real-time GPS fleet tracking (infrastructure incomplete)
- require integration into DPW's internal project management systems
- enforce sustainability design standard compliance
- depend on private operational data
- claim to predict project completion dates without official source confirmation
