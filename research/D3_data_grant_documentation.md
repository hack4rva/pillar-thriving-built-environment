# D³ Associates Program – Data Grant Documentation

*Digital Data Design Institute at Harvard – Official Grant Guide* 

--- 

## Executive Summary
The Digital Data Design (D³) Associates Program provides approximately $50,000 in funding for one year to Harvard University faculty conducting research on how artificial intelligence (AI) is reshaping business, organizations, and society [1] [2]. The program prioritizes large-scale field experiments, high-impact moonshot ideas, and pilot projects that deliver initial research insights [1]. Awardees are required to participate in quarterly cohort meetings, submit midterm and final reports, and collaborate with the D³ Institute to translate their research into practitioner insights and learning experiences [1]. All grant funds for the current cycle must be expended by December 31, 2026 [1].

---

## Program Overview
The **Digital Data Design (D³) Associates Program** accelerates Harvard faculty research on how artificial intelligence (AI) reshapes business, organizations, and society [2]. Funded projects receive approximately **$50,000** for **1 year** to conduct large-scale field experiments, moon-shot pilots, or early-stage insight generation [1]. The Institute provides quarterly cohort meetings, mentorship, and dissemination support, including insights articles, workshops, and industry outreach [1]. 

--- 

## Eligibility
- **Who can apply?** Harvard University faculty [1]. 
- **PI eligibility:** Must hold a Harvard faculty appointment; no limit on seniority. 
- **Multiple applications:** Applicants may apply for funding for multiple projects but can receive only one award [1]. 
- **Ongoing research:** Ongoing research projects may apply for funding if the proposal clearly demonstrates how the additional support will create unique insights or broader impact [1].

--- 

## Eligible Project Types
We invite proposals from Harvard faculty that advance knowledge on how AI is reshaping business [1]. Priority is given to:
- **Large-scale field-based experiments** with practical implications for industry [1]. 
- **High-impact moonshot ideas** [1]. 
- **Pilots** to deliver initial research insights [1]. 

Applications should be supported by a defined research proposal and design, including objectives, data sources, methodologies, and anticipated outcomes and impact [1].

## Selection Criteria & Review Process
While specific internal scoring rubrics are not publicly disclosed, successful proposals must clearly demonstrate:
1. **Relevance to AI-business impact** – advancing knowledge on how AI is reshaping business [1]. 
2. **Scientific rigor** – supported by a defined research proposal and design, including objectives, data sources, and methodologies [1].

## Data Access & Usage Terms
- **Research Insights:** Awardees will authorize the use of their AI-related research publications for at least three Insights articles during their award term [1]. 
- **New Work:** At least one Insight should be based on new work produced during the award term [1]. 
- **LLM Usage:** Full text of open access articles may be uploaded into LLMs for summarization purposes and to extract insights. For articles without open access, faculty may approve or deny this [1].
